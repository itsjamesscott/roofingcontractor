Why Timely Asphalt Shingle Repair Matters

  • Prevents water damage and mold growth inside your home

  • Extends the life of your roof, delaying costly replacement

  • Improves your home's curb appeal

  • Avoids structural damage to roofing underlayment and decking

Timely repairs preserve your investment and reduce disruptions caused by larger roofing problems.

Step 1: Inspect and Identify Damage

Before climbing onto your roof, inspect it safely from the ground using binoculars or from a ladder if comfortable. Look for:

  • Missing, cracked, curled, or broken shingles

  • Areas with granule loss exposing the underlying material

  • Signs of leaks or water stains inside your home's attic or ceiling

If damage is localized, you can proceed with repairs. Widespread issues may require professional assessment or a full roof replacement.

Step 2: Gather Necessary Tools and Materials

Prepare before you begin by collecting:

  • Replacement asphalt shingles matching your roof

  • Roofing nails (galvanized or stainless steel)

  • Hammer and pry bar

  • Utility knife

  • Roofing cement or sealant

  • Safety equipment (non-slip shoes, gloves, ladder stabilizer)

Having the right tools ready makes the job safer and more efficient.

Step 3: Prepare the Work Area

Safety is a priority. Choose a dry, calm day for the repair. Securely position your ladder and wear proper footwear. On the roof, gently clear debris like leaves, dirt, or loose shingles from the repair area to ensure good adhesion.

Step 4: Remove the Damaged Shingle

Using the pry bar, carefully lift the edges of shingles above the damaged piece. Remove nails holding the broken shingle by prying or pulling them out to avoid damaging adjacent shingles. Slide out the damaged shingle gently, exposing the underlayment. Inspect that area for damage, and if necessary, repair any minor tears with roofing cement.

Step 5: Trim and Fit the New Shingle

Measure the opening left by the removed shingle and trim a replacement shingle with the utility knife to fit perfectly. Align the grain and size so it blends seamlessly with existing shingles. Slide the new shingle into place, ensuring edges line up neatly with neighboring shingles.

Step 6: Secure and Seal the Replacement Shingle

Nail the new shingle securely using 4-6 roofing nails placed just below the adhesive strip. Press the shingle down firmly, then apply roofing cement beneath the edges of the adjacent shingles to seal and prevent wind uplift. This also helps waterproof the repair site.

Step 7: Final Inspection and Cleanup

Double-check that nails are flush and shingles lie flat. Clean up any debris or loose granules on the roof and in gutters to maintain proper drainage. Proper cleanup prevents clogs and prolongs roof life.

Bullet Points: Quick Tips from Evergreen Exteriors
